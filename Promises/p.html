Bubbling and capturing

Start
Event bubbling and capturing are ways events propagate through the DOM. Bubbling starts at the innermost element where the event occurs and travels up through parent elements. Capturing is the opposite, starting at the outermost element and moving down to the target. By default, events bubble up, and capturing is less commonly used.

Protypes

  • Every function has a prototype property.

  • You can add or modify properties and methods on the prototype after object creation.
  • Prototypes form a chain, allowing objects to inherit from multiple levels.

Closures

A closure is a function that has access to variables in its outer (enclosing) function's scope, even after the outer function has returned. In essence, it's a function bundled together with its lexical environment.

Super keyword
